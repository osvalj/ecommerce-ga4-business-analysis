## 1) Traffic Growth vs Revenue Decline

**File:** `traffic-vs-revenue.png`

This visualization highlights a strong year-over-year increase in traffic alongside a significant decline in total revenue. The divergence indicates that acquisition growth alone is not driving business performance and suggests inefficiencies in conversion, product mix, or user intent monetization. This insight motivates a deeper analysis beyond top-of-funnel metrics.

---

## 2) Channel Performance and Revenue Contribution

**File:** `channels-performance.png`

Cross-network channels generate the highest share of total revenue, while Paid Social stands out for its higher average revenue per purchase. This contrast suggests that some channels may be optimized for efficiency rather than scale, and that budget allocation decisions should consider profitability, not only traffic volume.

---

## 3) User Behavior and Content Engagement

**File:** `user-behavior-pages.png`

User behavior analysis shows strong engagement with specific product categories, particularly guitars, as well as frequent interaction with pages related to physical stores. While these pages attract attention and engagement, they do not consistently translate into conversions, indicating an opportunity to better align content, intent, and commercial outcomes.

---

## 4) Brand Performance and Private Label Opportunity

**File:** `brand-analysis.png`

The analysis reveals a misalignment between brand visibility, sales volume, and revenue contribution. The private label brand shows a relatively low revenue share but a higher-than-average conversion rate among exposed users. This suggests untapped growth potential driven by visibility and positioning rather than product performance issues.

---

## 5) Conversion Funnel and Drop-off Analysis

**File:** `conversion-funnel.png`

The conversion funnel shows very high abandonment rates, with only a small fraction of users completing a purchase, well below typical ecommerce benchmarks. This indicates that improving conversion efficiency and reducing friction in the checkout process could have a greater impact on revenue than increasing traffic acquisition.
